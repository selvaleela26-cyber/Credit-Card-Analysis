# Credit Card Financial Dashboard – Power BI
Project Overview 

The Credit Card Financial Dashboard is a data analytics project built using Power BI to analyze customer credit card behavior and financial patterns. The dashboard provides insights into customer spending habits, credit utilization, payment trends, and potential credit risk indicators.

Financial institutions generate a large volume of transaction data through credit card usage. Analyzing this data helps organizations understand customer behavior, monitor credit risk, and design better financial strategies.

This project focuses on transforming raw customer financial data into meaningful insights through interactive visualizations. The dashboard enables decision-makers to explore customer activity, spending distribution, and payment performance in a clear and structured way.

Project Objectives

The main objectives of this project are:

 * To analyze credit card spending behavior across different categories

 * To evaluate credit utilization compared to assigned credit limits

 * To study customer payment patterns and identify late payment trends

 * To detect potential credit risk indicators

 * To build an interactive Power BI dashboard for financial analysis

Dataset Description:

The dataset used in this project contains information related to credit card customers and their financial activity.

The dataset includes the following key attributes:

Column	              Description
Customer              ID	Unique identifier for each customer
Age	Customer          age
Gender	              Gender of the customer
Income Level	        Customer income category
Credit Card Type	    Type of credit card used by the customer
Credit Limit	        Maximum credit limit assigned to the customer
Total Spending	      Total amount spent using the credit card
Spending Category	    Category of spending such as travel, groceries, utilities, etc.
Outstanding Balance  	Remaining unpaid balance
Payment Date	        Date of payment made by the customer
Payment Status	      Payment behavior (on-time, delayed, missed payment)

These attributes help analyze customer demographics, financial behavior, and credit risk patterns.

Data Preparation and Cleaning :
Before building the dashboard, the dataset was processed and prepared in Power BI.

The following steps were performed:
* Data Import

 * The dataset was imported into Power BI from CSV or Excel format.

 * Data Cleaning

Data cleaning was performed to ensure accuracy and consistency:
 * Removed duplicate records

 * Handled missing or incomplete values

 * Standardized column names

 * Corrected inconsistent data entries

 * Data Transformation

The dataset was structured into meaningful fields to support analysis:
 * Age values were grouped into age segments

 * Spending transactions were categorized into spending groups

 * Payment records were classified into payment behavior categories

 * These transformations improved the usability of the dataset for dashboard visualization.

 * Dashboard Features

 * The Power BI dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of credit card customer behavior.

The key components of the dashboard include:
* Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

The dashboard highlights important financial metrics such as:
* Total Credit Limit

* Total Spending

* Total Outstanding Balance

* Average Credit Utilization

* Percentage of Late Payments
These KPI cards allow quick monitoring of financial performance.

Spending Analysis :
A category-wise spending analysis visualizes how customers distribute their expenses across different categories such as:
* Travel
* Entertainment
* Utilities
* Shopping
* Groceries
This helps identify the most common spending areas among credit card users.

Credit Utilization Analysis :
Credit utilization measures how much of the available credit limit is used by customers.

This visualization compares:
*Assigned credit limits
*Actual credit usage
*High credit utilization may indicate potential financial stress or credit risk.
*Customer Demographics Analysis
*Customer demographics are analyzed to understand how financial behavior varies across different groups.

Demographic insights include:
* Spending patterns by age group
* Credit usage by gender
* Financial behavior based on income levels
These insights help financial institutions design personalized financial products.

Payment Behavior Analysis :
Payment behavior analysis helps track how customers manage their credit card payments.

This section identifies:
* Customers who pay on time
* Customers who frequently delay payments
* Customers with missed payments
This information is important for credit risk monitoring and early warning systems.

Key Insights :
* Based on the analysis, several insights can be observed:
* Some spending categories contribute significantly more to overall credit card usage.
* Customers with higher credit limits tend to have higher spending levels.
* A portion of customers frequently show high credit utilization, which may indicate financial risk.
* Payment patterns reveal that while many customers pay on time, delayed payments still occur among certain segments.
These insights help financial institutions improve credit policies and customer financial support strategies.

Business Recommendations:
* Based on the analysis, the following recommendations can be considered:
* Introduce reward programs for high-spending categories to encourage customer engagement.
* Monitor customers with high credit utilization levels to prevent potential financial risk.
* Implement automated alerts for late payment patterns.
* Design personalized credit limits based on customer income and spending behavior.
* Provide financial awareness programs for customers with risky payment habits.
These actions can help improve customer financial management and reduce credit risk exposure.

Tools and Technologies Used :
* Power BI – Data visualization and dashboard development
* Excel / CSV – Data storage and dataset source
* Power Query – Data transformation and cleaning
* DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) – Creating calculated measures and KPIs
